About the SW Dice Roller

The Star Wars Dice Roller is designed to be used in conjunction with Fantasy Flight Games' Star Wars Role Playing Game. This website is not made with any input or coordination from Fantasy Flights, which still holds the copyright to all of their original content.


Core Rule Books (CRBs)
  • Edge of the Empire - Focuses on Smugglers, Bounty Hunters, Bantha Fodder, and Nerf Herders.
  • Force and Destiny - Jedis are the centerpiece of this CRB. Force powers galore.
  • Age of Rebellion - You know all those pilots and fighters that get killed in the movies? Be one of them (and maybe not die)!

Dice Types

In the Star Wars Dice Roller, the dice are mostly non-numerical. (There is one numerical die interface; it defaults to a D100.) Broadly speaking, the Boost, Ability, and Proficiency dice represent the likelihood of success, while the Setback, Difficulty, and Challenge dice represent the likelihood of failure. A typical dice pool will use some from each category.

  • Boost
  • Ability
  • Proficiency
  • Force
  • Setback
  • Difficulty
  • Challenge
  • Numerical
Pips

Each face of a Star Wars Die has 0, 1, or 2 pips. Success, Advantage, and Triumph are good results for the roller. Failure, Threat, and Despair are bad results for the roller. Light and Dark pips have their own set of rules.

  • Success
  • Advantage
  • Triumph
  • Light
  • Failure
  • Threat
  • Despair
  • Dark


Success and Failure

Success pips and Failure pips cancel each other. In order to succeed in a roll, the results must have one more Success pip than Failure pips. A roll with no uncancelled success pips is a failure.

Advantage and Threat

Advantage pips and Threat pips cancel each other. It is possible to succeed on a roll, but with threat, just as it is possible to fail with advantage. See the Core Rulebooks for how these results play out narratively.

Triumph and Failure

Triumph pips and Despair pips do not cancel each other. The effects of a Triumph can be compared to a "critical hit" that you may have seen in another system. Likewise, a Despair can be thought of as a "critical failure". In addition to these effects, each Triumph "includes" a Success, and each Despair "includes" a failure. These additional pips are accounted for by the dice roller.

Force Pips

Light side force pips and Dark side force pips do not cancel each other. They have their own unique rules for use. Please see one of the Core Rulebooks for how they are used.

Numerical Dice

In the Star Wars Role Playing Games, the Numerical dice tend to only be used in the determination of critical injuries to characters or ships.
